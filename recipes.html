Chicken Biryani


YIELD: 4 servings

This recipe really only makes two dinner-sized servings with one leftover, smaller lunch, hence the three servings listed in the nutritional values.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup cooked chicken, diced or shredded
  • 1 cup long grain or jasmine rice (cook and allow to cool)
  • 2 tbsp canola oil
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, finely chopped garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro (or parsley) finely chopped
  • 4 tsp Garam Masala
  • ½ tsp lite salt

PREPARATION

  • Preheat oven to 300º
  • Heat oil in wok over high heat and saute onions until golden.
  • Add ginger and garlic, stirring occasionally for 1 minute.
  • Add tomatoes and cook until dry and the tomatoes begin to melt, about 3 minutes.
  • Add Garam Masala and stir fry, stirring frequently for a minute or two, until the Garam Masala is very fragrant.
  • Add water, chicken, and lite-salt; reduce heat to low and simmer 3 to 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until water is well-blended.
  • In a glass, ovenproof dish, alternate layers of ½ rice and ½ chicken mixture.
  • Cover tightly with foil and bake for 30 minutes, allowing flavors to meld.
  • Prior to serving, mix well.

Nutritional Info: Calories 263.5; Total Fat 11.8g, Saturated Fat 1.3g, Polyunsaturated Fat 3.4g, Monounsaturated Fat 6.3g; Cholesterol 35.0mg; Sodium 227.4mg; Potassium 635.6mg; Total Carbohydrate 24.0g; Protein 15.6g


Shrimp Tacos with Fresh Corn Salsa


INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup fresh corn kernels (about 2 ears)
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon Nu-Salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 diced peeled avocado
  • Cooking spray
  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 teaspoons honey
  • 1/4 cup light sour cream
  • 8 (6-inch) corn tortillas

PREPARATION

  1. Preheat broiler to highest setting.
  2. Combine corn and oil in small bowl; toss gently to coat. Arrange corn in an even layer on a jelly-roll pan; broil 6 minutes or until lightly browned. Combine corn, onions, cilantro (if used), 1 tablespoon lime juice, salt, pepper, and avocado in a medium bowl; toss gently.
  3. Heat a large grill pan over medium-high heat. Coat pan with cooking spray. Combine shrimp, 1 tablespoon lime juice, and honey in a medium bowl; toss to coat. Add shrimp to pan; cook 2 minutes on each side or until done.
  4. Combine sour cream and remaining 1 tablespoon lime juice in a small bowl.
  5. Warm tortillas according to package directions. Place 2 tortillas on each of 4 plates. Top each tortilla with about 1/4 cup corn mixture, about 4 shrimp, and 1 1/2 teaspoons sour cream mixture.

Note: Soft corn tortillas generally have much less sodium than the hard taco shells and are prefered for this recipe although the crispy ones can be substituted.



Pico de Gallo


YIELD: 4 servings

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 medium tomato, diced
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1/2 fresh jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 2 sprigs fresh cilantro, finely chopped (Optional)
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon Nu-Salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon pepper

PREPARATION

  1. In a medium bowl, combine tomato, onion, jalapeno pepper (to taste,) cilantro, lime juice and green onion.
  2. Combine corn and oil in small bowl; toss gently to coat. Arrange corn in an even layer on a jelly-roll pan; broil 6 minutes or until lightly browned. Combine corn, onions, cilantro (if used), 1 tablespoon lime juice, salt, pepper, and avocado in a medium bowl; toss gently.
  3. Season with garlic powder, salt and pepper.
  4. Stir until evenly distributed.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.


Honey-Broiled Sea Scallops


YIELD: 4 servings

NOTE: Sea scallops are large, usually about 1 and 1/2 inches in diameter, while bay scallops average about 1/2 inch in diameter.

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tbsp canola oil
  • 1 tbsp orange blossom honey
  • 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 1 lb large sea scallops
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds

PREPARATION

  1. Combine lime juice, oil, honey, soy sauce, and ginger in a bowl. Add scallops and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  2. Remove scallops from marinade, reserving marinade. Thread scallops evenly on 4 skewers. Place the skewers on a shallow baking pan that has been sprayed with nonstick cooking oil.
  3. Broil 4 to 6 inches from heat source for 2 to 3 minutes. Turn and baste with reserved marinade and continue cooking for 2 to 3 minutes or until opaque throughout.
  4. Place sesame seeds on wax paper and roll each skewer over seeds to evenly coat scallops. Serve immediately.

NUTRITIONAL INFO: Calories 176; Total Fat 4.4 g; Saturated Fat 0.3 g; Polyunsaturated Fat 1.3g, Monounsaturated Fat 2.1g; Cholesterol 37 mg; Sodium 193.3 mg; Total Carbohydrates 7 g; Protein 20 g

I’ve discovered that soy sauce is another one of the challenges while on a low sodium diet. The ubiquitous Kikkoman® Soy Sauce has 920mg per tablespoon, while their Less Sodium Soy Sauce has 575mg!
